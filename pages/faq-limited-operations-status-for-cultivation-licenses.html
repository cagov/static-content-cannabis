Cultivation licensees may now place a cultivation license in Limited Operations Status.  

Q: How do I qualify for this status?

To qualify for Limited Operations Status, the licensee must pay any applicable licensing fee and meet all applicable license renewal requirements.  

Licensees with licenses in Limited Operations Status are prohibited from having mature plants on their premises and must destroy any mature plants not harvested before the date the license was placed in Limited Operations Status within 30 days.  

Q: What activities can be conducted when in Limited Operations Status?

Limited Operations Status allows a cultivation licensee to conduct the following activities:  

  • Process and sell cannabis harvested before entering Limited Operations Status, and 
  • Possess and maintain seeds and immature plants used solely for propagation to preserve the genetic lineage of the licenseeâ€™s cannabis plants. 

A cultivation license placed in Limited Operations Status must remain In Limited Operations Status for the duration of the Modified License Term.  The licensee may opt to return the license to active status at time of renewal.   

Q: How can I request this change? 

Eligible cultivation licenses can be placed in Limited Operations Status during license renewal or when requesting a one-time change to the license expiration date using the Cannabis Licensing System (CLS). 

View steps for using the CLS

Q: How will fees be calculated? 

When an eligible cultivation license is placed in limited operations status the licensee is responsible for paying the applicable application fee plus 20% of the Original Cultivation License fee.
