Tips for filing a complaint 

You can file an anonymous complaint against any cannabis business, licensed or unlicensed. Provide as much detail as you can. Please be aware that incomplete complaints and lack of contact information can prevent DCC from gathering additional information that could help lead to the resolution of a complaint.

DCC’s role in supporting the legal market 

The Department of Cannabis Control (DCC) partners with cities, counties, law enforcement and other state agencies to uphold California’s commercial cannabis laws and regulations. We support the integrity of the legal cannabis market through:

  • Regular inspections
  • Careful investigations
  • Coordinated enforcement actions

DCC’s Compliance and Enforcement Divisions works with law enforcement and other state agencies to enforce the law. Officers:

  • Investigate complaints of unlicensed or illegal cannabis activity
  • Stop the sale of cannabis to minors
  • Prevent unsafe products from entering the cannabis market

Human trafficking

If you suspect human trafficking at a licensed facility, we encourage you to file a complaint with both DCC and the Department of Industrial Relations. If you suspect human trafficking at an unlicensed facility, we recommend you file a report with your local law enforcement office.
