Nearly two-thirds of California cities and counties do not allow the establishment of any regulated cannabis retailers within their jurisdictions. This limits consumer access to regulated, tested cannabis and cannabis products.

To address this, the Retail Access Grant provides local governments with resources to develop and implement cannabis retailer licensing programs. $20 million in total funding is available, distributed in two phases. 

By supporting local governments in establishing cannabis retail licensing programs, this grant seeks to: 

  • Provide consumers with reliable access to regulated, tested cannabis 
  • Reduce demand in the illicit market 
  • Establish enough cannabis retail stores statewide to meeting existing consumer demand 

Funding rounds

Funding is distributed in two phases:

  • Phase I: provided over $4 million in funding to 18 cities and counties to develop and implement a local cannabis retailer licensing program. 
  • Phase II: awards funding to eligible cities and counties based on the number of local cannabis retailer licenses that they issue. 
    • Over $15 million in total funding is available in Phase II. 
    • An eligible jurisdiction may apply for funding in Phase II even if it did not participate in Phase I. 

Phase II funding opportunity 

Phase II of this program provides funding to eligible local governments based on the number of cannabis retail permits that they issue. 

Subject to available funds, the Department of Cannabis Control (DCC) intends to award up to the following amounts per local license issued: 

  • $150,000 for each eligible cannabis retailer license issued, or 
  • $300,000 for each eligible equity cannabis retailer license issued 

A maximum of $2 million per jurisdiction will be awarded in Phase II. 

Eligibility: Local jurisdictions (city, county, or city and county) that: 

  • Prior to July 1, 2022, did not have a cannabis retailer licensing program in existence; 
  • Has a plan to develop and implement a cannabis retail licensing program; and 
  • Has issued one or more cannabis retail licenses to cannabis businesses within the jurisdiction of the city or county pursuant to that program. 

Application Periods: Phase II applications will be accepted in the following months, subject to available funding: October 2023, March 2024, September 2024, March 2025, September 2025, March 2026. 

Grant materials

In August 2023, to better differentiate the phases and streamline the issuance of funds, DCC amended the original grant guidelines into two separate documents, one for Phase I and the other for Phase II.  

The Phase I Grant Guidelines now only provide the requirements for Phase I, which awarded jurisdictions funds to develop and implement local cannabis retailer licensing programs. The Phase II requirements are now contained in the Phase II Grant Guidelines which govern the issuance of funds based on the number of local retailer and equity retailer licenses issued.
