Follow these steps to safeguard yourself, your business, and your employees from robbery, burglary and theft.

Definitions

  • Robbery: Taking of items by force or fear, such as a bank robbery.
  • Burglary: Entering a building with the intent to commit a theft, such as a late night break-in.
  • Theft: Typically committed by employees, like stealing cash from a register.

Robbery

  • Maintain visibility into your place of business. Keep windows clear of signs or merchandise to avoid blocking the view into your business and keep all entrances, exits and parking areas well lit.
  • Install a hold-up alarm system.
  • Protect transactional areas. Place registers at a safe distance from the door and install physical barriers that are high enough to keep unauthorized persons out of the area where cash transactions take place.
  • Minimize cash on premises. Keep your on-premise safe out of sight and locked at all times.  Make frequent cash drops to keep cash on premises to a minimum. Use a courier service if you’re dropping cash off premises.
  • Use more than one person to open and/or close the business. There is safety in numbers.
  • Don’t hesitate to contact the authorities. Post law enforcement emergency and non-emergency phone numbers in a visible location. Call the police immediately if you see something suspicious.

Burglary

  • Secure the premises when closed. Install high-quality locks and check the condition of the locks on a regular basis. Instruct the last person who leaves the premises to lock all doors and windows and include this step in your written closing instructions.
  • Install a security system or burglar alarm. Choose one that includes video surveillance. Check your protection system to make sure it is working properly and have a backup power supply in case of an outage. 
  • Secure valuables. Remove all merchandise at the end of each day and place merchandise that can’t be removed in a safe.
  • Secure your safe. Keep it out of customer view and bolt it to the floor or find another way to prevent it from being moved.
  • Keep your windows clear. Don’t tint or obscure your windows. This allows burglars to act out of sight once they are inside your business. Apply a protective film to the windows to make them difficult to break or remove
  • Keep your building well lit. Burglars prefer to work in the dark, so installing motion lights on the roof and perimeter of your building can deter suspicious activity.

Theft

Most theft is committed by employees. 

  • Consider an employee policy of no handbags or backpacks, or only clear bags.
  • Review your video footage frequently.
  • Provide each employee with an individual alarm code.
  • Limit access to your safe.

Reminder: Theft must be reported to the DCC

If you do experience a theft or break-in, make sure to notify local authorities. You also must report the event to the Department of Cannabis Control (DCC) within 24 hours of the incident with form DCC-LIC-028 as required by section 15036 of the DCC regulations.   

If you have any questions or need further assistance, please contact us at info@cannabis.ca.gov.
