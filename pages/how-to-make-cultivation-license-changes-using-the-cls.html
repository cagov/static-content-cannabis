Looking to make changes to your cultivation license? Certain cultivation license changes, such as  reducing the size of a cultivation license, placing the cultivation license in Limited Operations Status, and making a one-time change to the expiration date of a processor, nursery, or cultivation license, can be handled through the Cannabis Licensing System (CLS).

Making one-time changes

Eligible licensees can now use DCC’s Cannabis Licensing System (CLS) to make a one-time change their license expirations date.  Additionally, eligible cultivation licensees can place their license in Limited Operations Status or request a Reduced-Size Cultivation License, when making the one-time request to change their license expiration date in the Cannabis Licensing System (CLS).   

Make one or more changes at time of renewal

Licensees can also take advantage of the new license change options during the license renewal period.

Support

For questions, related to using your CLS account to make one or more of these changes, please contact licensing@cannabis.ca.gov 

Background

On March XX, 2024, the Department of Cannabis Control (DCC) adopted emergency regulations to implement Senate Bill 833.  Under the new regulations, cultivation licensees can make certain changes to their license including:  

  • Reducing the size of a cultivation license, 
  • Placing the cultivation license in Limited Operations Status, and 
  • Making a one-time change to the expiration date of a processor, nursery, or cultivation license.
