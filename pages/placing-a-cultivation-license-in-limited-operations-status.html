Selecting this option allows cultivators the opportunity to temporarily pause cultivation commercial cannabis activity, including cultivation on their canopy, at a reduced cost until the next renewal.

Definition

Limited Operations Status means an inactive status.

Permitted

  • Engaging in the processing and sale of cannabis harvested before the status was changed.
  • Possessing and maintain seeds and immature plants used solely for propagation to preserve the genetic lineage.

Not permitted

  • Engaging in commercial cannabis activities, including the cultivation or possession of mature plants.

Overview

  • Limited Operations Status remains in effect until the next renewal.
  • At each subsequent renewal, cultivators may request to remain in Limited Operations Status.

Eligibility

  • Nursery and Processor licenses are excluded from being in Limited Operations Status.
  • Limited Operations Status cannot be combined with a request to reduce the canopy size of a license type.

Background

The Department has adopted emergency regulations to implement Senate Bill 833. Under these regulations, there are new options available for licensed cultivators renewing their license.

Criteria:

  • Your cultivation license expires on or after March 1, 2024
  • Changes are requested during your renewal period

These opportunities are voluntary and are selected at the discretion of the cultivator.
