Overview

  • DCC published initial grant guidelines for the Cannabis Local Jurisdiction Retail Access Grant Program on February 14, 2023
  • These guidelines included both Phase I and Phase II requirements
  • The guidelines were later split into separate documents to make it easier to understand the differences between each phase

Notable changes

Logistics

  • RetailAccessGrants@cannabis.ca.gov is the new contact email for the program. 
  • A local jurisdiction may apply for Phase II funding regardless of whether it received Phase I funding. Previously, participation in Phase I was a requirement for Phase II eligibility. 
  • Phase I grantees that fail to establish a local cannabis retailer licensing program by October 31, 2025, may still be eligible to apply for Phase II funds.  
  • Phase II applications will be accepted twice a year, subject to available funds

Eligibility

The new Phase II applicant eligibility criteria includes local jurisdictions (city, county, or city and county) that:  

  • Prior to July 1, 2022, did not have a cannabis retailer licensing program in existence; 
  • Has a plan to develop and implement a cannabis retail licensing program; and 
  • Has issued one or more cannabis retail licenses to cannabis businesses within the jurisdiction of the city or county pursuant to that program. 

Funding amounts

  • Phase II funding amounts for the issuance of retailer licenses increased to $150,000 per retailer license and $300,000 per equity retailer license issued, subject to available funds.

Applications

  • Removed the requirement that a local retailer licensee submit a complete state license application to DCC prior to the release of Phase II funding for the issuance of retailer licenses. 
  • Eligible local jurisdictions may receive awards in Phase II in connection with the following types of retail: adult-use storefront, adult-use delivery, and medicinal storefront. Additionally, jurisdictions that received Phase I funding may receive funding in connection with the issuance medicinal delivery licenses in Phase II. 
  • Jurisdictions applying for funding in Phase II must submit a Retail Access Grant Phase II Application (DCC-2633), Attachment A – License Report (DCC-2634), Attachment B – Budget (DCC-2635) with justification, and copies of all local cannabis retailer licenses issued. 
  • For both Phases, the restriction that approved expenditures only be incurred after the local jurisdiction enters into a Grant Agreement was removed. Upon execution of the grant agreement, eligible expenses incurred within the Grant Term (July 1, 2023 – June 30, 2026) may be included as activities to be funded by this award.
