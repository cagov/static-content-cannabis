The Department of Cannabis Control (DCC) operates offices in Northern, Central, and Southern California. At these locations, you can make cash or check payments, submit license and renewal fees, or check the status of a payment. Appointments are strongly recommended to ensure timely assistance.

General office hours

  • Monday–Friday, 8 a.m.–5 p.m.
  • Excluding state holidays

How to schedule an appointment

  1. Request an appointment: Email payments@cannabis.ca.gov or call the office directly (see above)
  2. Complete the application form: A one-page form is required; request and return it by email. A DCC representative can assist by phone if needed.
  3. Provide details: Share your preferred date/time and business information.
  4. Receive confirmation: Both the customer and license owner(s) will receive a confirmation email — required before visiting.

Appointment tips

  • Special cases: Cash collection appointments on Friday afternoons are by special request only.
  • Renewals reminder: Online license renewals must be submitted before your payment appointment to avoid late fees.
  • Missed appointments: Customers must reschedule if an appointment is missed.

Payment tips

  • Complete online renewals first: Submit renewals online before your appointment.
  • Correct payee: Make checks, money orders, or cashier’s checks payable to “DCC” or “California Department of Cannabis Control”.
  • Include details: Note what the payment is for (e.g., license renewal, application) and include business information.
  • Use tracking for mailed payments: Strongly recommended for checks, money orders, and cashier’s checks.
  • No wire transfers: DCC does not accept wire payments.

Appointment policies

  • Confirmation required: You must receive a confirmation email before coming to your appointment.
  • Cancellations or changes: Contact payments@cannabis.ca.gov or call 1-844-61-CA-DCC (1-844-612-2322).
  • Drop-ins: Limited availability before 4 p.m. (Mon–Thu in Eureka & North Hollywood; Mon–Wed in Rancho Cordova). Not guaranteed — appointments are best.
  • Late arrivals: More than 10 minutes late may require rescheduling, depending on availability.

Payment questions

For all payment related questions, a response will be provided within 48 hours of the request. If we are unable to respond within the 48-hour timeframe, we will contact you concerning the delay.
