DCC licenses and regulates all commercial cannabis activity in California. This includes the growing, manufacturing, distribution, testing, and sale of cannabis and cannabis products.  

DCC can also help route complaints about human trafficking, which is a complex issue that requires the involvement of multiple agencies.

You can file an anonymous complaint against licensed or unlicensed cannabis businesses. Provide as much detail as you can. If the complaint is incomplete or we cannot contact you to gather additional information, we may not be able to investigate the complaint.

How DCC’s Enforcement Division helps…

DCC’s Compliance and Enforcement Divisions works with law enforcement and other state agencies to enforce the law. Officers:

  • Investigate complaints of unlicensed or illegal cannabis activity
  • Stop the sale of cannabis to minors
  • Prevent unsafe products from entering the cannabis market

If you suspect human trafficking at a licensed facility, we encourage you to file a complaint with both DCC and the Department of Industrial Relations. If you suspect human trafficking at an unlicensed facility, we recommend you file a report with your local law enforcement office.
