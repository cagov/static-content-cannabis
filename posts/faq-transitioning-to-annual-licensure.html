No provisional license will be effective after January 1, 2026, with exception to locally verified equity retailers pursuant to Business and Professions Code (BPC) section 26050.5, subdivision (c). 

If a provisional license does not transition to an annual license prior to its 2025 expiration date, the license must cease operations per California Code of Regulations (CCR), title 4, section 15024.1.  Licensees that possess cannabis or cannabis products on the premises must take the following actions:

  • The cannabis or cannabis products may be destroyed by the former licensee; or
  • A licensed distributor or licensed microbusiness authorized to engage in distribution may submit a written request form (DCC-LIC-028) to the Department within 14 calendar days of the former licensee’s termination to be authorized to procure and distribute the former licensee’s entire inventory stock in accordance with CCR, title 4, section 15024.1.

Frequently asked questions

Q:  How can I tell if I have an annual license or a provisional license?  

A:  After January 1, 2025, DCC will no longer renew a provisional license pursuant to Business and Professions Code section 26050.2, subdivision (o).  To determine if you have an annual or provisional license, you can look up your license information through instructions available in CLEaR system by clicking here, and in CLS by clicking here. 

Q: If I have a provisional license and it expires after January 1, 2025, can I still renew? 

A:  Provisional licenses must transition to annual licenses prior to their 2025 expiration in order to be eligible for license renewal, with the exception of locally verified equity retailers as provided by Business and Professions Code (BPC) section 26050.5, subdivision (c).

Q:  How can I learn more about transitioning my provisional license to an annual license?   

A:  A provisional licensee must comply with all annual license requirements for the applicable license type to transition a provisional license. Instructions to resolve any outstanding annual license requirements are provided below.  

CLS licensing system users: 

  • The DRP must reference all deficiency notice(s) and make sure to submit all final documentation within a Science Amendment. To learn more about submitting a Science Amendment, visit the DCC website.  
  • To locate your deficiency notice(s), the DRP must log into your account on the DCC’s cultivation licensing portal, select My Licenses, locate and select your license number, select Record Info, and then click Attachments.   
  • If you have already submitted a Science Amendment and have questions regarding the status, please email info@cannabis.ca.gov. 

CLEaR licensing system users:  

  • Reference all emailed deficiency notice(s) and email all final documentation to the DCC contact within the notice.   

If you already submitted all final documentation and have questions regarding the status, email info@cannabis.ca.gov. 

Q:  Are there any exceptions to the January 1, 2025, deadline?  

A:  Yes, locally verified equity retail licenses with valid provisional licenses may be eligible for provisional license renewal until January 1, 2031, or until 5 years from the date the provisional license was originally issued, whichever is earlier.  

For more information about renewing locally verified equity retail provisional licenses, review Business and Professions Code section 26050.5.   

Q:  What if I fail to transition to an annual license before I expire in 2025? 

A:  Failure to transition to annual licensure before provisional license expiration in 2025 may result in:

  • Your provisional license being suspended, revoked, cancelled, or naturally expired. If your license is no longer valid, you must cease all commercial cannabis activity immediately.  You will not be able to receive or transfer cannabis or cannabis products to or from the premises.  
  • Your annual application may be denied if you have not completed all annual license requirements by your expiration date. Please be aware that you may withdraw the application at any time prior to the denial of the license. If DCC denies your application for annual licensure, you may request an appeal hearing within 30 calendar days of the notification of denial.  

Q:  Can I continue to operate after the January 1, 2025, renewal deadline? 

A:  If your provisional license has not transitioned to an annual license and it is not expired, suspended, revoked, or cancelled, you may continue to operate unless you have been notified by DCC of other restrictions.  

Q:  If DCC is considering suspending or revoking a provisional license, what are the next steps?  

A: DCC will issue a Notice of Provisional License Review (Notice). The Notice will describe:  

  • The nature and facts of each violation;
  • The manner in which the provisional licensee must correct the violation(s) to achieve compliance;
  • That the Department is considering suspending, revoking, or denying the renewal of the provisional license.

The notice will provide instructions on how the provisional licensee may request an informal meeting with DCC to discuss the matter.  

Q: If my provisional license is not transitioned prior to my license expiration date, what close out activities are required?

A: If your provisional license is terminated for any reason, all cannabis and cannabis products should be sold or destroyed by the time the license is no longer active. If the license is terminated for any reason and cannabis or cannabis products remain on the premises, a distributor or microbusiness authorized for distribution may obtain prior approval from DCC to buy and distribute the former licensee’s inventory. A licensed distributor or licensed microbusiness authorized to engage in distribution shall, within 14 calendar days of the termination of the former licensee’s license, submit a written request to the Department, on the Licensee Notification and Request Form, Notifications and Requests Regarding Regulatory Compliance, by submitting a form DCC-LIC-028. Refer to CCR, title 4, section 15024.1 for further instruction.

Q:  Who can I contact if I have questions? 

A:  A license’s DRP can email info@cannabis.ca.gov for more information.  Please include the license number in question.
