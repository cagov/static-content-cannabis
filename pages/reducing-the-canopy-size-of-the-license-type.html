Selecting this option allows the cultivator to scale down their production by reducing their canopy size and corresponding license fees until the next renewal.

Definitions

  • An “Original Cultivation License” is the cultivation license type indicated on the license prior to any changes.
  • A “Reduced-Size Cultivation License” is a cultivation license type specified by the cultivator that satisfies both of the following:
    • Maximum canopy size is smaller than the original.
    • Lighting type remains the same as the original.
Overview
Eligibility
  • Nursery, Processor, and Specialty Cottage licenses are not eligible for a license size change.
  • A license size change cannot be combined with a request to be placed in Limited Operations Status.
  • Lighting type changes are not permitted.
  • Cultivators cannot change to a license size that is larger than their original cultivation license.
Renewal documentation requirement
  • Submit an updated premises diagram that satisfies all the following:
    • Meets the requirements listed in the California Code of Regulations section 15006;
    • Identifies the original canopy area; and
    • Identifies the canopy area for the proposed reduced-size cultivation license. This area shall be located within the canopy area of the original cultivation license.
Provisional Licenses
  • Provisional license holders must continue to pursue annual licensure regardless of license size.
  • Cultivators can choose to pursue annual licensure for either the original cultivation license or a reduced-size cultivation license.
    • If the cultivator chooses to pursue annual licensure for a reduced-size cultivation license, the provisional licensee must be engaged with the scientist reviewing their CEQA documentation.
    • Once the provisional license transitions to an annual license, the annual license size becomes the original cultivation license.
Fees
  • The fee is based on the reduced-size cultivation license.
  • Fees are listed in the California Code of Regulations sections 15014 and 15014.2.

How to request this option 

  1. Review the regulations and the licensing system renewal guidance document.
  2. Login to your CLS account.
  3. Select the license you want to renew.
  4. Input your desired selections and any requested information.
  5. Upload required documentation, if applicable.
  6. Pay the license fee.
  7. Submit your renewal request. 

Background

The Department has adopted emergency regulations to implement Senate Bill 833. Under these regulations, there are new options available for licensed cultivators renewing their license.

Criteria:

  • Your cultivation license expires on or after March 1, 2024
  • Changes are requested during your renewal period

These opportunities are voluntary and are selected at the discretion of the cultivator.
