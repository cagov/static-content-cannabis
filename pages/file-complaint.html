DCC licenses and regulates commercial cannabis activity in California. A complaint can be submitted if an individual or business is suspected of violating DCC’s laws and regulations.

If a complaint does not fall within DCC’s regulatory jurisdiction, we may refer the complaint to the appropriate local, State, or federal agency on your behalf. Issues outside of DCC’s regulatory jurisdiction may be related to:

  • Environment
  • Labor
  • Taxes
  • Discrimination
  • Animal welfare
  • Business disputes
  • Human trafficking

Learn more about where to direct complaints on issues not within DCC’s regulatory jurisdiction.

How to file a complaint 

Our analysts can investigate a complaint more thoroughly when sufficient details are provided, such as “who, what, when, where, and how.” Include:

  • Narrative describing the nature of the complaint
  • Business name, phone number, address, and license number, if applicable
  • Names of individuals involved and/or witnesses
  • Date(s) of witnessed activities related to the complaint
  • Description of observations, concerns, possible violations
  • Documentation including photos, receipts, timecards, certifications, or copies of email or other communications. 
File a complaint

Complainants are required to provide contact information to allow DCC to gather any additional information about the complaint. Although contact information is required, there is the option to remain anonymous and DCC will not publicly disclose the complainant’s contact information to the extent authorized by law.

What to expect after you file a complaint

After a complaint has been submitted, DCC analysts will determine if the issue falls within DCC’s regulatory jurisdiction or should be referred to a partner agency. For matters within DCC’s primary jurisdiction, the analysts will review the complaint to determine if the activity violates regulations and the subsequent course of action.

The complainant will not be informed of the outcome of their complaint nor can DCC provide information on open investigations.
