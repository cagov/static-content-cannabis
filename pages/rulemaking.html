Regulations are state laws with specific rules for how businesses and others must operate.  Regulations are created and changed through a process called rulemaking. Changes must be approved by the Office of Administrative Law (OAL).

The Department of Cannabis Control (DCC) is taking steps to simplify the cannabis regulations.  View DCC’s proposed and completed rulemaking below.

Regulatory notices

Section 280 Certification of CCTT System Changes – Transport and Destination Approvals

On November 20, 2023, the Department notified licensees of upcoming functionality changes to CAT 118, related to Transport and Destination Approvals.  However, due to an Underground Regulation Petition filed with the Office of Administrative Law, the Department has determined it will not issue, use, enforce, or attempt to enforce the CAT 118 Transport and Destination Approvals update.  The determination to not proceed with the CAT 118 update was Certified to the Office of Administrative Law on Monday, February 5, 2024.

How regulations are made

Regulations are made through a process called rulemaking. It gives the public the opportunity to take part in the creation of regulations proposed by California state agencies. Learn more about the rulemaking process.
