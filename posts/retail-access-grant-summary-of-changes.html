Overview

  • DCC published initial grant guidelines for the Cannabis Local Jurisdiction Retail Access Grant Program on February 14, 2023
  • These guidelines included both Phase I and Phase II requirements
  • The guidelines were later split into separate documents to make it easier to understand the differences between each phase

Notable changes

Logistics

  • RetailAccessGrants@cannabis.ca.gov is the new contact email for the program. 
  • A local jurisdiction may apply for Phase II funding regardless of whether it received Phase I funding. Previously, participation in Phase I was a requirement for Phase II eligibility. 
  • Phase I grantees that fail to establish a local cannabis retailer licensing program by October 31, 2025, may still be eligible to apply for Phase II funds.  
  • Phase II applications will be accepted twice a year, subject to available funds

Eligibility

The new Phase II applicant eligibility criteria includes local jurisdictions (city, county, or city and county) that:  

  • Prior to July 1, 2022, did not have a cannabis retailer licensing program in existence; 
  • Has a plan to develop and implement a cannabis retail licensing program; and 
  • Has issued one or more cannabis retail licenses to cannabis businesses within the jurisdiction of the city or county pursuant to that program. 

Funding amounts

  • Phase II funding amounts for the issuance of retailer licenses increased to $150,000 per retailer license and $300,000 per equity retailer license issued, subject to available funds.

Applications

  • Removed the requirement that a local retailer licensee submit a complete state license application to DCC prior to the release of Phase II funding for the issuance of retailer licenses. 
  • Eligible local jurisdictions may receive awards in Phase II in connection with the following types of retail: adult-use storefront, adult-use delivery, and medicinal storefront. Additionally, jurisdictions that received Phase I funding may receive funding in connection with the issuance medicinal delivery licenses in Phase II. 
  • Jurisdictions applying for funding in Phase II must submit a Retail Access Grant Phase II Application (DCC-2633), Attachment A – License Report (DCC-2634), Attachment B – Budget (DCC-2635) with justification, and copies of all local cannabis retailer licenses issued. 
  • For both Phases, the restriction that approved expenditures only be incurred after the local jurisdiction enters into a Grant Agreement was removed. Upon execution of the grant agreement, eligible expenses incurred within the Grant Term (July 1, 2023 – June 30, 2026) may be included as activities to be funded by this award. 
Summary of Specific Changes to the Phase I Grant Guidelines 
  • The document title was changed from “Grant Guidelines” to “Phase I Grant Guidelines” to specify that these guidelines now apply to Phase I. A separate set of grant guidelines will be published for Phase II of the grant program. 
  • The document date was changed from February 2023 to August 2023 to reflect the month the new document was published. 
  • The Table of Contents was adjusted to properly reflect the changes to the document. 
  • References to $20 million being available for award were changed to $10 million to reflect the amount that is available for Phase I. An additional $10 million, including any unused funds from Phase I, will be available for award in Phase II. The overall funding available for award under this program remains unchanged at $20 million.  
  • Changes were made to clarify that the Phase I Grant Guidelines apply to Phase I. References to Phase II were removed. 
  • Removed the date referencing when Phase II funding will become available for award – this will be addressed in the Phase II Guidelines.  
  • A new date in the timeline was added to indicate when the original Grant Guidelines were amended to the Phase I Grant Guidelines.  
  • The email address grants@cannabis.ca.gov, to be used by applicants when communicating with DCC regarding the grant program was changed to RetailAccessGrants@cannabis.ca.gov where necessary – the new email DCC is utilizing for this program. 
  • Removed references to Phase II, including Phase II available funding and the references to Phase II in the example fund disbursement breakdown. 
  • Clarified that detailed explanations and justifications for all proposed expenditures are required. 
  • Removed the requirement that approved expenditures only be incurred after the local jurisdiction enters into a Grant Agreement. Upon execution of the grant agreement, eligible expenses incurred within the Grant Term (July 1, 2023 – June 30, 2026) may be included as activities to be funded by this award. 
  • Added a notice to applicants that materials submitted to DCC may be subject to disclosure under the California Public Records Act. 
  • Removed language stating the DCC will announce grant awards in “early” June as grant awards were announced on June 20. 
  • Removed reference to the “Scope of Work” in the Grant Agreement section to specify that submitted application materials, including clarifications provided during the review process, will comprise the Grant Agreement. 
  • Removed “Phase II Fund Disbursement” section outlining how Phase II funds will be disbursed. This is now captured in the Phase II Grant Guidelines.  
  • Changed “application” to “grant agreement” regarding the recapture of funds not expended by June 30, 2026. 
  • Added “as prescribed in the Budget Act of 2022” in reference to recaptured funds being returned to the General Fund, as “general fund” is not defined in the Grant Guidelines.  
  • Changed the reporting timeframes for the Annual Progress Reports. Previously, the report was due on December 1 but would have included the whole month of December in the reporting period. Changing the reporting periods will avoid a jurisdiction having to submit an annual progress report on December 1 even though the reporting period went through to December 31. The first Annual Report will now be due January 1, 2024, instead of December 1, 2023. 
  • Removed the stipulation that Phase I grantees that fail to establish a local cannabis retailer licensing program by October 31, 2025, will not be eligible for Phase II funds.  
  • Added language to the Terms and Conditions defining “Grant Agreement,” “Grantees,” “DCC,” “Parties,” and “Party” for clarity. 
  • Clarified that, for the purposes of the Terms and Conditions, unless otherwise specified, all definitions, rules, guidelines, and requirements in the Grant Guidelines shall apply to the Agreement.  
  • Changed “Department” to “DCC” in the Terms and Conditions for clarity. 
  • Changed the first provision in the Terms and Conditions to prohibit the use of grant funds for expenditures that occur outside of the expenditure period of July 1, 2023, through June 30, 2026, instead of prohibiting expenditures made prior to approval of the grant agreement.  
Summary of Specific Changes to the Phase II Grant Guidelines 
  • The document title was changed from “Grant Guidelines” to “Phase II Grant Guidelines” to specify that these guidelines apply to Phase II. A separate set of grant guidelines will be published for Phase I of the grant program. 
  • The document date was changed from February 2023 to August 2023 to reflect the month the new document was published. 
  • The Table of Contents was adjusted to properly reflect the changes to the document. 
  • References to $20 million being available for award were changed to “over $15 million” to reflect the amount of funding available for Phase II after completion of the Phase I awards. The overall funding available for award under this program remains unchanged at $20 million.
  • Changes were made to clarify that the Phase II Grant Guidelines apply to Phase II. Most references to Phase I were removed as this document now covers Phase II only. The Phase I requirements are now covered in the Phase I Grant Guidelines. 
  • References to the ‘question and answer’ period were removed. Local jurisdictions may contact DCC at any time with questions regarding Phase II. 
  • The final application deadline was changed to March 31, 2026, at 5:00 p.m. This is the final deadline of the last Phase II application period. 
  • The applicant eligibility criteria were changed to be more specific to Phase II activities and the issuance of local cannabis retailer licenses. Under the original grant guidelines, to be eligible for Phase II funding, a jurisdiction must have received funding in Phase I. This limitation was removed. The new eligibility criteria are as follows: 
  • Eligible applicants: Local jurisdictions (city, county, or city and county) that:
    • Prior to July 1, 2022, did not have a cannabis retailer licensing program in existence; 
    • Has a plan to develop and implement a cannabis retail licensing program; and 
    • Has issued one or more cannabis retail licenses to cannabis businesses within the jurisdiction of the city or county pursuant to that program. 
  • The amount of grant funding available for the issuance of cannabis retailer and equity cannabis retailer licenses was increased. Under the original grant guidelines, a jurisdiction could be awarded $75,000 for the issuance of a retailer license and $150,000 for the issuance of an equity retailer license. Under the new Phase II Grant Guidelines, a jurisdiction may receive $150,000 per retailer license and $300,000 per equity retailer license issued, subject to available funds. The $2 million maximum per jurisdiction in Phase II remains. 
  • References to the establishment of cannabis retailer licensing programs were changed to the issuance of cannabis retailer licenses, as that is the award trigger mechanism for Phase II. 
  • Clarified that the expenditure period for Phase II grants is July 1, 2023, through June 30, 2026. 
  • The timeline was revised to remove references to Phase I activities and to include specific dates for Phase II activities.
  • The email address, “grants@cannabis.ca.gov”, to be used by applicants when communicating with DCC regarding the grant program was changed to “RetailAccessGrants@cannabis.ca.gov”– the new email DCC is utilizing for this program. 
  • Added language clarifying if a jurisdiction is unsure whether it is eligible for Phase II funding that it may contact DCC at RetailAccessGrants@cannabis.ca.gov.
  • Removed language regarding jurisdictions that have already begun program development, as it is not applicable to Phase II. 
  • Removed examples of eligible and ineligible local jurisdictions as they are no longer applicable in Phase II. 
  • Removed references to Phase I, including Phase I available funding and the references to Phase I in the example fund disbursement breakdown. 
  • The “Funding Rounds” section was changed to “Phase II Funding”; references to Phase I funding were removed. 
  • References to Phase I funding amounts were removed. 
  • The “Example Fund Disbursement” section was edited to just show an example of what Phase II funding might look like, subject to available funds. 
  • “License Types Eligible for Award” section was added to clarify which types of retailer licenses are eligible for Phase II award. To be eligible for grant award, a retailer permit must be issued to a premises located within the jurisdiction of the city or county. Eligible local jurisdictions may receive awards in Phase II in connection with the following types of retail: adult-use storefront, adult-use delivery, and medicinal storefront. Clarified that, additionally, jurisdictions that received Phase I funding may receive funding in connection with the issuance medicinal delivery licenses. 
  • Under the “Eligible Uses and Costs” section, clarified that local jurisdictions must use funding to support the continued development and implementation of a cannabis licensing program (including the issuance of cannabis retailer licenses), or for programs that directly support cannabis equity businesses. 
  • Made small, technical conforming changes throughout the document. 
  • Clarified that grant funding provided in Phase II for the issuance of an equity license must be used for the benefit of the cannabis equity program or cannabis equity businesses. 
  • Updated the application requirements to reference the specific application forms required to apply for Phase II funding. Jurisdictions applying for funding in Phase II must submit a Retail Access Grant Phase II Application (DCC-2633), Attachment A – License Report (DCC-2634), Attachment B – Budget (DCC-2635), and copies of all local cannabis retailer licenses issued. References and descriptions of the Phase I application materials were removed. 
  • Specified that upon execution of the grant agreement, eligible expenses incurred within the Grant Term (July 1, 2023 – June 30, 2026) may be included as activities to be funded by this award. 
  • Updated the “Application Submission Process” section to clarify the requirements for Phase II applications. A local jurisdiction may apply for Phase II funding regardless of whether it received Phase I funding.
  • Added the “Phase II Application Periods” chart to clarify the six Phase II application periods: October 2023, March 2024, September 2024, March 2025, September 2025, and March 2026. The final Phase II application deadline is March 31, 2026, at 5:00 p.m. A jurisdiction may apply for funding during multiple application periods but only one application per jurisdiction per application period will be accepted. 
  • Added a notice to applicants that materials submitted to DCC may be subject to disclosure under the California Public Records Act. 
  • Clarified that grant applications will be disqualified is they are submitted outside of an application period. 
  • Clarified the application processing order and removed references to priority funding that only applied to Phase I funding. Phase II applications will be processed in the order they are received until funding is exhausted. 
  • Clarified that DCC will announce Phase II grant awards after each application period.
  • Removed reference to the “Scope of Work” in the Grant Agreement section to specify that submitted application materials, including clarifications provided during the review process, will comprise the Grant Agreement. 
  • Removed language that would have required an executed Grant Agreement be in place before expenditures may begin.
  • Removed original Appendix B regarding priority jurisdictions; renamed Appendix C to Appendix B; Renamed Appendix D to Appendix C. 
  • Specified that signed grant agreements, payee data forms, and approved governing board resolutions or authorizations must be returned to DCC prior to release of funds. Clarified that DCC, in its sole discretion, may withdraw and redirect funds if documents are not returned within 60 days. For funds awarded in the final application period in March 2026, jurisdictions must return documents to DCC as soon as possible but no later than June 1, 2026. 
  • Clarified that no funds will be disbursed after the end of the grant term on June 30, 2026. 
  • Clarified how Phase II funding will be disbursed – including that awarded funds will be distributed to local jurisdictions in a single payment, rather than as an 80-20 split as occurred with Phase I. 
  • Removed the requirement that a local retailer licensee submit a complete state license application to DCC prior to the release of Phase II funding for the issuance of retailer licenses. 
  • Clarified that approved Phase II awards will be processed for payment in the months following the application periods. 
  • Changed “application” to “grant agreement” regarding the recapture of funds not expended by June 30, 2026. 
  • Added “as prescribed in the Budget Act of 2022” in reference to recaptured funds being returned to the General Fund, as “general fund” is not defined in the Grant Guidelines.
  • Changed the Annual Progress Report to an annual Expenditure Report (DCC-2636); cited new form number; slightly modified when the Expenditure Report must be submitted. 
  • Struck references to the Phase I Closeout Report which is not relevant to Phase II. 
  • Changed the definition of an “Eligible local jurisdiction” in Appendix A: Definitions to match the eligibility requirements specific to Phase II. 
  • Removed all of Appendix B regarding jurisdictions eligible for priority; changed old Appendix C to Appendix B, and old Appendix D to Appendix C. 
  • Added language to the Terms and Conditions defining “Grant Agreement,” “Grantees,” “DCC,” “Parties,” and “Party” for clarity. 
  • Clarified that, for the purposes of the Terms and Conditions, unless otherwise specified, all definitions, rules, guidelines, and requirements in the Grant Guidelines shall apply to the Agreement.
  • Changed the first provision in the Terms and Conditions to prohibit the use of grant funds for expenditures that occur outside of the expenditure period of July 1, 2023, through June 30, 2026, instead of prohibiting expenditures made prior to approval of the grant agreement.
  • Changed “Department” to “DCC” in the Terms and Conditions for clarity. 
  • Modified the sample resolution found in new Appendix C to make it specific to Phase II and the issuance of retailer licenses, rather than the establishment of a retailer licensing program as occurred in Phase I.
