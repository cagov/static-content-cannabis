Complaints handled by other organizations

DCC works closely with local, state, and federal partners on issues surrounding cannabis regulation that do not fall under our jurisdiction but that impact the market. Below is a list of issues that we recommend you direct to other agencies.

Complaints handled by partner organizations:

Discrimination

Discrimination is the act of making distinctions between people based on the groups, classes, or other categories to which they belong or are perceived to belong. People may be discriminated on basis of race, gender, age, religion, disability, or sexual orientation, as well as other categories.

  • Complaints of discrimination may be referred to the CA Civil Rights Department (formerly the CA Department of Fair Employment and Housing) at: https://calcivilrights.ca.gov/

Environmental

  • Illegal Timber Harvest complaints are handled by the California Department of Forestry and Fire Protection. The California Department of Forestry and Fire Protection (CAL FIRE) enforces the laws that regulate logging on privately-owned lands in California.  You will need to contact a regional office, a listing can be found here: https://www.fire.ca.gov/programs/resource-management/forest-practice/timber-harvesting/timber-harvesting-plan-thp/ 
  •  Negative impact of on wildlife allegations are handled by the California Department of Fish and Wildlife. Members of the public remain our best source of information related to illegal cannabis cultivation. If you witness a polluting or poaching incident, a fish and game code violation, or have information about a violation, call CalTIP 1-888-334-CALTIP (888-334-2258) or https://wildlife.ca.gov/Enforcement/CalTIP 
  •  Odor nuisance, light pollution or noise complaints are generally handled by your local city or county (depending on the local jurisdiction) Code Enforcement Department. 
    Find your county’s website information here:  https://www.counties.org/county-websites-profile-information. Or find your city’s website information here: https://www.counties.org/cities-within-each-county 
  •  Pesticide Violation allegations are handled by the local county Agricultural Commissioner’s Office.  To find your local ag commissioner please go here: https://www.cdfa.ca.gov/exec/county/countymap/.  
    For more information on pesticide related claims please visit the California Department of Pesticide Regulation: https://www.cdpr.ca.gov/docs/dept/quicklinks/report.htm 

Expired lease

  • Local Better Business Bureau (BBB) (www.bbb.org) and local consumer agency (which can be found DCA’s website as they have a list of all consumer agencies by county in CA): https://www.dca.ca.gov/consumers/dispute_resolution_programs.shtml 

Labor

  • Failure to maintain a safe workplace or provided mandated training allegations are referred to the California Department of Industrial Relation’s Division of Occupational Safety and Health (DOSH), better known as Cal/OSHA is responsible for regulating the protections of California workers and improving workplace health and safety.  https://www.dir.ca.gov/dosh/Complaint.htm 
  •  Failure to properly pay workers is a matter that is regulated by the California Department of Industrial Relations Division of Labor Standards Enforcement (DLSE).  DLSE adjudicates wage claims on behalf of workers who file claims for nonpayment of wages, overtime, or vacation pay, pursuant to California Labor Code. https://www.dir.ca.gov/dlse/HowToFileWageClaim.htm 
  •  Failure to meet labor-peace-agreement requirements allegations are handled by the California Agricultural Labor Relations Board (ALRB).  These complaints are handled by the ALRB.   https://www.alrb.ca.gov/e-file/ 

Returns, refunds, and exchanges

  • Local Better Business Bureau (BBB) (www.bbb.org) and local consumer agency (which can be found DCA’s website as they have a list of all consumer agencies by county in CA): https://www.dca.ca.gov/consumers/dispute_resolution_programs.shtml

Taxation

Complaints alleging a licensed entity has failed to pay taxes. 

  • Cannabis Excise or Sales Tax matters are referred noted and referred to the California Department Fee and Tax Administration (CDTFA).   https://cdtfa.ca.gov/rptfraud.htm 
  • Payroll or Employment Tax matters are referred to the California Employment Development Department (EDD).  https://askedd.edd.ca.gov/AskEDD/s/categorydetails?category=Report_Fraud
