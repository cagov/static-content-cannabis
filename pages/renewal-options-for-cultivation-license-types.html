The Department has adopted emergency regulations to implement Senate Bill 833. Under these regulations, there are new options available for licensed cultivators renewing their license.

Criteria:

  • Your cultivation license expires on or after March 1, 2024
  • Changes are requested during your renewal period

These opportunities are voluntary and are selected at the discretion of the cultivator. Select an opportunity to learn more.

Place the license in limited operations status
Reduce the canopy size of the license type
Make a one-time change to the current renewal date

Place a cultivation license in limited operations status

Selecting this option allows cultivators the opportunity to temporarily pause cultivation commercial cannabis activity, including cultivation on their canopy, at a reduced cost until the next renewal.

Definition

Limited Operations Status means an inactive status.

Permitted

  • Engaging in the processing and sale of cannabis harvested before the status was changed.
  • Possessing and maintain seeds and immature plants used solely for propagation to preserve the genetic lineage.

Not permitted

  • Engaging in commercial cannabis activities, including the cultivation or possession of mature plants.

Overview

  • Limited Operations Status remains in effect until the next renewal.
  • At each subsequent renewal, cultivators may request to remain in Limited Operations Status.

Eligibility

  • Nursery and Processor licenses are excluded from being in Limited Operations Status.
  • Limited Operations Status cannot be combined with a request to reduce the canopy size of a license type.
Overview
  • Limited Operations Status remains in effect until the next renewal.
  • At each subsequent renewal, cultivators may request to remain in Limited Operations Status.
Eligibility

  • Limited Operations Status cannot be combined with a request to reduce the canopy size of a license type.
Fees
  • The fee is 20% of the applicable annual license fee.
  • Fees are listed in the California Code of Regulations sections 15014 and 15014.2.

Reduce the canopy size of the license type

Selecting this option allows the cultivator to scale down their production by reducing their canopy size and corresponding license fees until the next renewal.

Definitions

  • An “Original Cultivation License” is the cultivation license type indicated on the license prior to any changes.
  • A “Reduced-Size Cultivation License” is a cultivation license type specified by the cultivator that satisfies both of the following:
    • Maximum canopy size is smaller than the original.
    • Lighting type remains the same as the original.
Overview
Eligibility
  • Nursery, Processor, and Specialty Cottage licenses are not eligible for a license size change.
  • A license size change cannot be combined with a request to be placed in Limited Operations Status.
  • Lighting type changes are not permitted.
  • Cultivators cannot change to a license size that is larger than their original cultivation license.
Renewal documentation requirement
  • Submit an updated premises diagram that satisfies all the following:
    • Meets the requirements listed in the California Code of Regulations section 15006;
    • Identifies the original canopy area; and
    • Identifies the canopy area for the proposed reduced-size cultivation license. This area shall be located within the canopy area of the original cultivation license.
Provisional Licenses
  • Provisional license holders must continue to pursue annual licensure regardless of license size.
  • Cultivators can choose to pursue annual licensure for either the original cultivation license or a reduced-size cultivation license.
    • If the cultivator chooses to pursue annual licensure for a reduced-size cultivation license, the provisional licensee must be engaged with the scientist reviewing their CEQA documentation.
    • Once the provisional license transitions to an annual license, the annual license size becomes the original cultivation license.
Fees
  • The fee is based on the reduced-size cultivation license.
  • Fees are listed in the California Code of Regulations sections 15014 and 15014.2.

Make a one-time change to the renewal date

Selecting this option allows the cultivator to permanently modify the renewal date one time to provide more flexibility for when fees are due.

Definition

  • “Modified License Term” means-
    • A timeframe that is less than 12 months; and
    • Falls between the original and the modified date of renewal. 
Eligibility
  • All cultivation license types, including Nursery and Processor, are allowed to modify their renewal date one time.
  • Modification of the renewal date is only allowed once per cultivation license and becomes permanent once the renewal is paid and approved.
Fees
  • Cultivators will receive a prorated annual license fee for the Modified License Term.
    • The prorated fee is calculated by dividing the applicable annual license fee by 365 and multiplying that value by the number of days between the date the original and modified renewal date.

Formula-

(Existing Annual Fee / 365)  x Number of Days = New Annual Fee

Example-

Small Outdoor license type = $4,820 annual fee

Current Expiration Date = March 1, 2025

Modified Expiration Date = November 1, 2025

Calculation-

($4,820 / 365) x 245 = $3,235.34

Prorated Fee-

New Annual Fee = $3,235.34
Equity Fee Deferrals

If you have obtained an Equity Fee Deferral, you are required to pay all license fees in full within six months of the deferral being granted or prior to the modified renewal date, whichever is sooner.

How to request these options 

  1. Review the regulations and the licensing system renewal guidance document.
  2. Login to your CLS account.
  3. Select the license you want to renew.
  4. Input your desired selections and any requested information.
  5. Upload required documentation, if applicable.
  6. Pay the license fee.
  7. Submit your renewal request.
