DCC encourages you to file a complaint if you encounter an issue with a cannabis-related business. While some issues should be filed directly with DCC, we also work closely with local, state, and federal partners on issues surrounding cannabis regulation.

Discrimination

Discrimination complaints

What is discrimination?

Discrimination is the act of making distinctions between people based on the groups, classes, or other categories to which they belong or are perceived to belong. People may be discriminated on basis of race, gender, age, religion, disability, or sexual orientation, as well as other categories.

Agency to contact: CA Civil Rights Department

Environmental

Illegal timber harvesting

Who to contact:

Timber complaints are handled by the California Department of Forestry and Fire Protection. The California Department of Forestry and Fire Protection (CAL FIRE) enforces the laws that regulate logging on privately-owned lands in California. 

How to get started:

Find a regional office through the CAL FIRE website listing.

Negative impacts on wildlife

Who to contact:

Negative impact of on wildlife allegations are handled by the California Department of Fish and Wildlife. Members of the public remain our best source of information related to illegal cannabis cultivation.

How to report an incident:

Report poaching or pollution by completing the online form or call CalTIP: 1-888-334-CALTIP (888-334-2258).
Odor nuisance, light pollution or noise complaints

Who to contact

Odor nuisance, light pollution, or noise complaints are generally handled by your local city or county (depending on the local jurisdiction) Code Enforcement Department. 

How to start a claim

Find a county website or find a city website to get started.
Pesticide violations

Who to contact

Pesticide Violation allegations are handled by the local county Agricultural Commissioner’s Office. 

How to make a complaint

Find your local ag commissioner to start the complaints process.

For more information on pesticide related claims please visit the California Department of Pesticide Regulation.

Labor

Workplace safety and training issues

Who to contact:

Failure to maintain a safe workplace or provided mandated training allegations are referred to the California Department of Industrial Relation’s Division of Occupational Safety and Health (DOSH), better known as Cal/OSHA is responsible for regulating the protections of California workers and improving workplace health and safety.

How to get started:

File a workplace complaint online.
Failure to pay workers

Who to contact:

This is a matter that is regulated by the California Department of Industrial Relations Division of Labor Standards Enforcement (DLSE).  DLSE adjudicates wage claims on behalf of workers who file claims for nonpayment of wages, overtime, or vacation pay, pursuant to California Labor Code.

File a wage claim online.
Labor-peace agreements

Agency: California Agricultural Labor Relations Board (ALRB)

File a complaint with ALRB

Business complaints

Report an unlicensed business

Who to contact:

The Department of Cannabis Control

Types of issues handled:

Suspect illegal behavior or an unlicensed cannabis retail store?

You can file an anonymous complaint against any cannabis businesses, licensed or unlicensed. Please provide as much detail as possible.

How to file a complaint:

  • Collect all your information, including specific details like names, locations, and any physical evidence such as photos or screenshots.
  • Complete the online form to file an official complaint with DCC
  • Make sure to print or write down the case number provided after completing the form.
