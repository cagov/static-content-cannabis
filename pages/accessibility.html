The Department of Cannabis Control (DCC) is committed to digital accessibility and ensuring all Californians can access its services. We continually look for ways to improve the accessibility of our website.

Please direct complaints regarding language access to: info@cannabis.ca.gov

Website accessibility certification

The Department of Cannabis Control (DCC) publishes its Website Accessibility Certification on this page to provide transparency and ensure the public can easily access our certification record. This replaces our previous PDF-based certification process and aligns with how other state agencies share their accessibility commitments.

This page will be updated every two years as part of the state’s bi-annual certification requirement. Past certifications will be archived and remain available for reference.

The undersigned certify that, as of July 1, 2025, the website of the Department of Cannabis Control is designed, developed, and maintained to be accessible. This denotes compliance with the following:

  • California Government Code Sections 7405, 11135, and 11546.7
  • Web Content Accessibility Guidelines 2.1 published by the Web Accessibility Initiative of the World Wide Web Consortium
  • At a minimum Level AA success criteria
